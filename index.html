A junior studying Computer Science at Boston University.

My main area of interest and current focus is Computer Security. Learned about Network and Web Security, in CS58, where one of the projects was to apply SQL injection, XSS, CSRF to a provided vulnerable web app. Another project was to analyze the security of a real website, my group analyzed Netflix. Nowadays I'm exploring reverse engineering, and malware analysis.

Other areas of interest include High Performance Computing, and Artificial Intelligence. I worked in the HPC Lab at Washington University where I analyzed the viability of porting a database application to work on the Intel Xeon Phi Coprocessor by analyzing the performance of running a simple database application.

Currently, I'm serving as the education director (2015-) for Global App Initiative, a student club at BU that builds mobile apps (Android, iOS) for non-profit organizations. My job is to run the education committee that is in charge of holding workshops open to the BU community, provide resources to learn about mobile development, and to provide help and guidance to our members during office hours.
